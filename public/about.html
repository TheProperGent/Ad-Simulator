The Genius Behind
Ad Simulator

"I didn't disrupt the advertising industry. I obliterated it, rebuilt it, and put my name on the rubble."

About Ad Simulator Origin Story

It came to me during my third consecutive breakfast meeting — I was eating a smoked salmon bagel, surrounded by advisors whose sole purpose is to agree with me, when I asked the question that would change everything: what if watching advertisements was fun? The room fell silent. Someone wept. A junior analyst fainted. I took that as confirmation that I was onto something enormous.

Ad Simulator is the culmination of that vision: a web application so brilliantly conceived that users actually want to watch ads. Not because they have to. Not because a video is loading. But because they have been gently conditioned — through the miracle of gamification — to associate advertisements with reward, excitement, and the warm glow of personal achievement. You're welcome, Madison Avenue.

The platform allows real brands and advertisers to upload video or banner ads into a curated library. Users sign in, earn credits by watching those ads, and collect them like trading cards. It is, in the humblest possible assessment, the most important development in the history of digital media. Second only to the internet itself, which — had I been born ten years earlier — I almost certainly would have invented.

"Other people build apps. I build ecosystems. There is a difference, and that difference is me." — The Founder, to a room of increasingly uncomfortable investors

How It Works The Mechanics

The process is almost insultingly straightforward, which is of course the hallmark of true genius. A user creates an account, signs in with their Google credentials, and is immediately presented with a single, pulsing button: RUN AD. They press it. An advertisement plays. They are rewarded with one credit. This loop — stimulus, action, reward — has been a cornerstone of behavioral psychology since Pavlov, and I am not ashamed to say I borrowed it. Pavlov, wherever he is, should be honored.

Credits accumulate in the user's account across sessions. A global leaderboard ranks users by lifetime credits earned, total ads collected, and — most critically — by how many rare and legendary ads they have discovered. Because not all ads are created equal. This brings us to the crown jewel of my intellectual output: the Rarity System.

The Rarity System Stroke of Genius

Every time a user watches an ad, the platform secretly rolls the dice. Six tiers of rarity determine which ad is served — from the humble Common, tumbling out at a 60% chance, all the way up to the impossibly elusive Mythic, appearing so rarely that witnessing one should be considered a life event. I have been told by multiple therapists that I derived more satisfaction designing this system than from most human relationships. I consider that a compliment.

When a user collects a new ad for the first time, the platform erupts in a celebratory overlay — sparkles, color, fanfare. If it is an Epic or above, the visual effects escalate dramatically. I designed the Mythic reveal to be, in my own words, "aggressively joyful." My UX team had concerns. I overruled them. I am always right about these things.

The Collection User Library

Every ad a user watches is saved to their personal collection. Duplicates are permitted — even encouraged — because quantity badges (×2, ×3, ×4 and beyond) demonstrate dedication, and dedication is something I respect almost as much as revenue. Users can replay any ad in their collection at will, without earning additional credits, because I am generous but I am not reckless.

The collection view is organized by rarity, adorned with the appropriate glow effects, and serves as a permanent record of a user's advertising journey. When I look at a full collection, I see not just ads — I see a portrait of a person who made excellent choices. Starting with the choice to use this platform.

For Advertisers The Opportunity

Brands and advertisers gain access to the Admin Panel, where they can upload video ads or design rich banner creatives, assign them a rarity tier, and deploy them directly into the pool. An ad assigned Legendary rarity becomes, by definition, a luxury item — seen by only the most persistent users, coveted by the rest. This is scarcity marketing at its most elegant, and it was my idea, and I will be mentioning it in my memoir.

Ad performance is tracked in real time. Firestore cloud infrastructure ensures that every impression, every collection, every credit earned is logged with the precision of someone who takes data very, very seriously. That someone is me.

"The greatest trick the advertising industry ever pulled was convincing people ads were an interruption. I have corrected this. Ads are now the product. You're welcome." — The Founder, accepting an award he nominated himself for

Why This Matters The Legacy

We are living through a crisis of attention. People skip ads, install blockers, look away. The industry has responded by making ads louder, more intrusive, more desperate — a strategy I find both ineffective and deeply undignified. Ad Simulator represents an alternative philosophy: make the ad itself worth wanting. Make watching it feel like winning.

I did not set out to fix advertising. I set out to build something that would make me unfathomable amounts of money while also being genuinely clever. The fact that it may also rehabilitate the public's relationship with digital advertising is, I will admit, a satisfying bonus. Though not as satisfying as the money.

Ad Simulator is free to use. The platform runs on Cloudflare's global infrastructure, backed by Firebase authentication and real-time cloud data, which means it is fast, reliable, and — most importantly — scalable to the enormous audience I fully expect to arrive once word gets out. It will get out. It always does, when the idea is this good.
